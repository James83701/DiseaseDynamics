Overview
Disease transfer in urban settings is primarily influenced by high population density, which facilitates rapid spread. Urban areas experience more severe outbreaks compared to rural areas. Pathogens like Dengue are more common in cities, while Malaria is prevalent in rural areas. Better healthcare infrastructure in urban areas can mitigate some risks, but disparities in health literacy and access to care influence disease spread. Socioeconomic factors, such as social inequality and poor living conditions, also contribute to higher transmission rates. Effective disease control in cities relies heavily on education and awareness about prevention.

Synanthropic animals that thrive in urban areas often carry zoonotic diseases.
